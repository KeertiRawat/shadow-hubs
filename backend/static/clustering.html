What is this chart? Each bubble is a country in the global oil trade network for the selected year. The x-axis shows the shadow hub score (betweenness centrality residual) — how structurally central a country is as a trade intermediary beyond what its trade volume would predict. The y-axis (log scale) shows OFAC sanctioned entity count. Bubble size reflects total trade volume. Color indicates the clustering pattern.

Weighted clustering coefficient: For each country i, this measures the fraction of triangles among its trade partners, weighted by trade value. Formally, if i trades with j and k, a weighted triangle exists when j also trades with k. Computed via nx.clustering(G, weight='log_weight') on the directed trade graph per year.

Why it matters: Shadow hub detection identifies which countries are anomalous intermediaries, but not how they broker oil. The clustering coefficient reveals the brokerage structure:

  • Low CC: Partners don't trade with each other — the hub is a sole bridge between disconnected groups (exclusive broker).
  • High CC: Partners form a dense clique — oil circulates within a tight, possibly coordinated ring (laundering syndicate).

Filter: Only countries with OFAC entities > 5 and positive shadow residual appear in the ranked table, ensuring focus on sanctioned-exposure intermediaries.
